Coffee Sleeve

The aim of this project was to identify and improve a point of interaction related to coffee. The sleeve is designed to offer improved grip and thermal protection for takeaway coffee. The project started by creating a cultural probe kit which was designed to find out how the participant used a Starbucks coffee sleeve, and prompted the participant to report faults or areas of improvement for the coffee sleeve.

The cultural probe kit was designed to mimic a coffee sleeve, and offered 3 material samples and prompted the user to leave feedback about which material they felt offered the best insulation and grip properties. The results of the cultural probe showed that participants found that the sleeve did not allow enough coverage for the entire hand span, was not thick enough to fully insulate the hand, and did not allow enough grip for larger cups. The participants reported that the ideal material would be double ply cardboard in order to provide the best thermal insulation and grip.

The sleeve prototype is designed to increase coverage with less material, by using an ergonomic shape which reduces material in areas where the hand does not come into contact with the sleeve. The sleeve also features a fold back tab which allows extra thermal insulation by creating an area beneath the thumb with twin ply thickness.

A measurement chart was also designed to offer different sized sleeves for various hand spans and cup sizes. The chart offered a simple interaction of placing the hand over a template in order to establish which sleeve size would be the best fit.
