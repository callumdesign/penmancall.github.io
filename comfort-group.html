The cradle - binaural audio prototype.

This project was completed in cooperation with Jacob Marsh, Caela Heke and The Comfort Group and Victoria University of Wellington. The purpose of the project was to create a tangible interaction around sleeping. The concept was based around the effects of binaural audio stimulation and its efficacy for encouraging relaxation. The concept was based on studies sourced from The Journal of Physiology & Behavior (Volume 63, Issue 2, January 1998, Pages 249â€“252), Faculty of Mechanical Engineering and Robotics University of Science and Technology of Krakow Poland, and Music, science, and the rhythmic brain : cultural and clinical implications by Jonathan Berger and Gabe Turow.

As a group we developed a wall mounted speaker system which provided a range of binaural frequencies to encourage a restful and relaxed state before sleeping. The Cradle projects audio in a sorround sound fashion in order to create stereo sound which is crucial in ensuring that the binaural effect is achieved. Our prototype was 3D printed, however production models could be CNC routed from wood to offer a range of luxurious finishes. We also used Arduino in order to create a working prototype. The binaural effect was triggered by a touch sensor mounted beneath the fabric surface.
